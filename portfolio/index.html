02

How I work

I am continuing to develop my design process through SEG3125 — Analysis and Design of User Interfaces . My approach is simple: understand the user and the task, study the relevant design principles, apply them deliberately, and then test whether the resulting interface actually makes the experience easier.

→

Learn

Study the user, task, domain, and relevant principles from trusted course and UX resources.

→

Apply

Make deliberate choices and explain why each interaction, layout, colour, and word supports the user.

→

Build

Turn the design into a responsive and interactive interface with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, and Bootstrap.

→

Evaluate

Test the experience against user goals, course principles, usability heuristics, and feedback, then refine it.

What I am learning from

  • Nielsen Norman Group UX research, usability heuristics, and visual-design principles
  • Bootstrap 5 & W3Schools HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and interface components
  • SEG3125 @ uOttawa Analysis and Design of User Interfaces
